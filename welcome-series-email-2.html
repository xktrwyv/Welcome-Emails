Before the war, Olesia and Mykola Hnatiuk were raising their young sons in Kyiv. “We had apple and pear trees; the kids had a playground,” Olesia shared. “That was our life.”

Then the war began.

“When there is a siren, you grab the children,” Olesia said. The family started sleeping on the floor of a hallway, away from windows. It didn’t take long to realize there was no safe place left. “Nowhere was safe.”

Their journey of resilience and hope is captured in One Good Reason, a short-documentary that follows the Hnatiuks as they flee Ukraine and find safety in rural Unity, Wisconsin—thanks to Bryce and Maxine Luchterhand, the American couple who stepped forward to sponsor them.

With their sponsors’ support, the Hnatiuks found jobs, enrolled their boys in school, and began rebuilding their lives in a new community.

Today, Ukrainians like the Hnatiuks—and other refugee newcomers who found safety through legal sponsorship pathways—re once again facing uncertainty due to recent policy and program changes.
