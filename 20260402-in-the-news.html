Yesterday, the Supreme Court heard arguments in one of the most consequential immigration cases in decades—one that could redefine who gets to be American.

At issue is an executive order that would limit birthright citizenship based on the immigration status of the child’s parents, including for families here on temporary but legal pathways like humanitarian parole or H-1B visas. Get a simple overview of the case here.

For families across the country, the stakes are deeply personal. Imagine a child born in the United States—growing up in an American school, speaking English as their first language, knowing no other home—only to be told they are not a citizen of the country they were born into. That is the reality the outcome of this case could create for thousands of children.

The case, Barbara v. Trump, challenges more than a century of precedent affirming that anyone born on U.S. soil is a citizen. A decision is expected by June.
