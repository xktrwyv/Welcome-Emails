This month marks four years since Russia’s invasion of Ukraine, a painful milestone.

It's a moment to honor the bravery and resilience of Ukrainians worldwide and the extraordinary Americans who stepped up to welcome families fleeing war.

Through legal sponsorship pathways, and the support of Americans across the country, Ukrainian newcomers found safety here. But today, that safety is in jeopardy. Severe processing delays now threaten their ability to remain lawfully in the United States.

You can make a difference by voicing the need for faster processing of re-parole applications to your elected representative, ensuring Ukrainians remain safe.

Act now

Your voice has made a difference before.

When the federal government paused renewal processing for Ukrainians in the humanitarian parole program, Welcomers spoke out to Congress and in the courts. Because of that pressure, the pause was lifted.

Approvals surged. Families were able to stay. They were spared the unthinkable—being forced back to an active war zone.

But now, many are facing a new wave of uncertainty. Once again, families are trapped in delays. Once again, their futures are uncertain. And this time, many risk detention, not because they did anything wrong, but because of administrative backlogs.

Families are caught in major processing delays with real and tragic consequences. Ukrainian newcomer and father of three, Yurii Ahieiev, recently shared that he and his family are scrambling for solutions as they fear deportation despite applying for re-parole. We have heard of many cases like this, as Ukrainians have applied for re-parole, paid the fee, but never heard back—and now their legal status in the United States has expired.

Lawmakers need to hear clearly and urgently that the problem has not been fixed.

That’s why we’ve updated our Contact Your Representative form so you can reach out directly to your member of Congress and ask that re-parole be issued to Ukrainians in a fair and timely manner. Please share this message with other Americans you know who care about supporting our Ukrainian neighbors, and ask them to contact their elected representatives as well.
