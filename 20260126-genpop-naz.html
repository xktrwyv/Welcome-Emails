Dear {{{Recipient.FirstName}}},

This month marks one year since a wave of federal policy changes reshaped how newcomers are welcomed—and, in many cases, whether they are welcomed at all. Over the past year, the systems meant to help people build stable, secure lives in this country have weakened. For many families who have rebuilt their lives here, their futures feel uncertain at best.

Recent news, including what’s happening in urban centers like Minneapolis, adds to that uncertainty, leaving many with questions. To help provide clarity, we want to share with you what your fellow Welcomers and the people they’ve welcomed are experiencing. Many newcomers who arrived through legal pathways and followed all the rules are being apprehended and detained. Within 24–48 hours, they’re put on flights to detention facilities in Texas. The only recourse their Welcomer friends have is to file legal paperwork for their release.

Because of these increased detainments, many newcomers no longer feel safe going to work or school as they fear the arrival of law enforcement at their front doors. This is a credible fear that they’ve never faced before in our country.

With many feeling deeply unsafe leaving their homes, Welcomers are stepping up to deliver groceries, medicine, and other essentials; pay rent to ensure newcomers don’t lose their homes while they can’t go to work; and even cover legal expenses when they are wrongfully detained. We invite our Welcomer community to help.

Donate today

When you donate, 100% of funds will go toward meeting these urgent needs—food, medicine, and household essentials—for newcomers through the Welcome Fund.

Welcome.US, through the Welcome Fund, has long provided support to newcomers and the volunteers assisting them. Now, we’re making funds available to our community as they support each other through these hard times and newcomers await returning to work and everyday activities.

By showing up as a steadfast Welcomer, alongside others in our community and the newcomers they welcomed, you send a powerful message: You belong here, and you don’t have to do this alone.

It may surprise you to know that you are part of a community of over 2.8 million Welcomers. You represent every corner of the country—red, blue, purple, rural, urban. At a time when our country feels deeply politically divided, especially around welcoming newcomers, it feels important to share that Welcomers are conservatives, moderates, progressives, and people who don’t fit neatly into any political box, but who believe in showing up for their neighbors. And your will to welcome endures. Welcomers like you continue to show up—even as the work gets harder—because you have first-hand knowledge of how these changes are impacting the people you welcomed.

It gives me great hope that when given a choice between human beings in need and our politics, this community chooses welcoming over fear and community over division.
