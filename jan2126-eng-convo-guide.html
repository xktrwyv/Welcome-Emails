Over the last week, a series of policy shifts and expanded immigration enforcement actions have created greater instability and confusion for newcomers and the communities that have welcomed them. We’re sharing the latest updates and what they mean.

Here’s what we’re tracking right now:

  • In Minnesota, federal immigration agents have begun detaining refugees who are lawfully present in the U.S. in order to re-review their immigration status. These are individuals who have already gone through extensive vetting and background checks to be admitted as refugees.
  • This development follows an announcement earlier this year that U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) would begin re-examining all refugee approvals from Jan. 21, 2021, to Feb. 20, 2025, starting with cases in Minnesota.
  • Reporting indicates at least 100 Minnesotans with refugee status have been detained and sent to Texas, though the reasons for their detention have not been clearly explained.

These actions are unfolding as our community also marks one year since private sponsorship programs were shut down—a moment that continues to shape how Welcomers and newcomers alike experience uncertainty and unanswered questions.
