This month marks four years since Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. It's a painful milestone. It’s also a moment to recognize the bravery of Ukrainians worldwide, as well as the life-changing impact of Americans who’ve welcomed those Ukrainians forced to flee their homes due to the war. Ukrainian newcomers found safety through legal sponsorship pathways and the support of Americans across the country, but their ability to remain safely in the U.S. is in jeopardy due to significant processing delays.

You can make a difference by voicing the need for faster processing of applications to your elected representative, ensuring Ukrainians remain safe.

Act now

Your voice has impacted lawmakers before: The federal government's prior pause in processing renewal applications for Ukrainians who arrived through the humanitarian parole sponsorship program has been lifted! This is thanks to the incredible efforts of Welcomers who voiced their support to Congress and through the courts.

We saw a surge of approvals immediately after the pause was lifted, offering those Ukrainians continued safety from being forced to return to an active war zone. But today, many are facing a new wave of uncertainty as they risk detention just because of administrative processing delays.

Contact your rep

Families are caught in major processing delays with real and tragic consequences. Ukrainian newcomer and father of three, Yurii Ahieiev, recently shared that he and his family are scrambling for solutions as they fear deportation despite applying for re-parole. We have heard of many cases like this, as Ukrainians in his and other communities have applied for re-parole, paid the fee, but never heard back—and now their status has expired.

Lawmakers need to hear clearly and urgently that the problem has not been fixed.

That’s why we’ve updated our Contact Your Representative messaging to reach out directly to your member of Congress to ensure re-parole is issued to Ukrainians in a fair and timely manner. We’ve drafted a message for you that addresses parole delays to ensure Ukrainians can remain lawfully and safely in the U.S.

Contact your rep

Ukrainian newcomers are contributing to communities across the country: working in small businesses, strengthening local institutions, paying taxes, and building new lives. Welcoming them has strengthened America, too.

Four years after the invasion, the need for safety endures. So does the generosity of the American people. Americans want this nation to be defined not by fear, but by welcome. Our policies should reflect that truth. Please share this message with other Americans you know who care about supporting our Ukrainian neighbors.

Thank you for standing with Ukrainians, then and now.

With gratitude,
The Welcome.US Team
